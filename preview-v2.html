About Tanya

This work was not built as theory first.

It was built on snapped ligaments, sensory overwhelm, a brain that could not understand instruction manuals written in typical language, and a heart that broke over and over again.

And then I had a daughter who was built like me. And I decided: not on my watch.

What came next was forged in clinical practice, neurodivergent-affirming support, community design, leadership, and advocacy, until a framework emerged that actually worked, for people who were never the whole problem.

Matriarch. Viking. Misfit. Clinician. Scholar. Human. Birther of the Enduring. Author of ENOUGH.

Speaking

If capable people keep struggling in your system, the problem is probably not the people.

Good intentions do not prevent harm. When you cannot see the full load someone is carrying, you cannot give them what they actually need, no matter how much you want to help.

First, understand what it costs them to be here. Be the coach the person in front of you actually needs. Not the coach you were trained to be for someone else.
