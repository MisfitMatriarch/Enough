ENOUGH

Tanya Hicks

You have worked too hard for life to still be this hard.

You tried the therapy. The routines. The boundaries. The books. You kept doing what you were told would work. And it is still harder than it has any right to be.

ENOUGH is not another self-help book that tells you to try differently. It is the book that shows you why the trying kept failing. And what was actually happening the whole time you blamed yourself for the gap.

The exhaustion is not evidence of failure. It is evidence of a mismatch between how you are designed and the conditions you are operating in. The gap between your effort and your outcome is not a character problem. It is a structural one. And structural problems have structural solutions.

It does not let you off the hook. It shows you which hook you are actually on. It separates the weight that was never yours from the patterns that are. It names what has been happening. The invisible taxes, the wrong map, the advice built for someone else's architecture. And it shows what becomes possible when you stop fighting your own design and start building from it.

About Tanya

This work was not built as theory first.

It was built on snapped ligaments, sensory overwhelm, a brain that could not understand instruction manuals written in typical language, and a heart that broke over and over again.

And then I had a daughter who was built like me. And I decided: not on my watch.

What came next was forged in clinical practice, neurodivergent-affirming support, community design, leadership, and advocacy, until a framework emerged that actually worked, for people who were never the whole problem.

Matriarch. Viking. Misfit. Clinician. Scholar. Human. Birther of the Enduring. Author of ENOUGH.

First, understand what it costs them to be here. Be the coach the person in front of you actually needs. Not the coach you were trained to be for someone else.
