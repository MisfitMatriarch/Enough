The central question

Who gets to decide what a person’s life should look like?

That is sovereignty. My work is not about changing people. It is about creating the conditions where people can safely become their authentic selves.

That is why these words keep appearing:

  • Access
  • Safety
  • Choice
  • Voice
  • Belonging
  • Capacity
  • Consent
  • Holding
  • Understanding
  • Partnership

They are all prerequisites for sovereignty.

What my work is really about

I teach the conditions.

  • I do not actually teach authenticity.I teach the conditions that make authenticity possible.
  • I do not teach confidence.I teach the conditions where confidence naturally grows.
  • I do not teach independence.I teach environments where interdependence does not remove agency.
  • I do not teach regulation.I teach access.
  • I do not teach resilience.I teach conditions that reduce unnecessary suffering.

Human beings flourish when they have genuine authority over their own lives, within relationships and communities that can hold them.

The work moves outward

From the person, into systems.

01

The person

Understanding your experience restores your ability to make informed choices instead of living inside everyone else’s interpretations.

02

Relationships and support

Support should never replace a person’s voice. It should increase their influence over their own life.

03

Professional practice

Professionals must ask whether a person has genuinely had access before interpreting, assessing or intervening.

04

Systems

Good practice must be held through supervision, teams, organisations and systems that remain accountable to the person.

About me

I am an Autistic author and practitioner, a counsellor and Behaviour Support Practitioner, a framework developer, researcher and speaker.

I founded Neurodivergent Empowered and Sparkly Aliens Inc., and I am the author of the wider Free 2 B Me body of work. My work asks a different question of practice - not “what can this person do?”, but “what conditions are shaping what we’re able to see?”

Counsellor (ACA R82053), NDIS Behaviour Support Practitioner (P1362818), and Master of Counselling candidate. I created the Sovereignty Conditions Audit.

The deeper question remains

Who gets to decide what a person’s life should look like?

People make sense in context. Behaviour makes sense. Distress makes sense. Changes in capacity make sense.

Support should not be designed to make people appear more compliant, independent or typical. It should reduce unnecessary barriers, protect rights, support communication and build the conditions in which people can participate, recover, develop and belong.
